Program Delivery · 2021 – 2023

G6 LIMS Multi-Site Production Deployment

Managed the phased rollout of Data Mine's G6 PROD2 LIMS across four NSW coal testing laboratories — Muswellbrook, Mount Thorley, Wollongong, and Newcastle. Scope ran end to end: server infrastructure setup, migration of legacy sample and result data, network configuration, and role-based training for 100+ technicians, analysts, and supervisors on registration, result entry, and reporting.

Every sample follows the same six-stage lifecycle a modern LIMS is built around — register, schedule, prepare, analyze, quality-control, and report — with barcode/QR tracking and an audit trail stamped at every step. Rolling that lifecycle out consistently across four independently-run sites, then configuring it to meet NATA ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation (audit trails, role-based access with segregation of duties, e-signatures, automated chain-of-custody), was the real scope of this program.

Industry context: ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation typically spans 20–30 individual criteria documents covering staff competency, equipment traceability, and data integrity — configuring a LIMS to satisfy all of them without slowing daily throughput is the hard part of any accredited-lab rollout.

Systems Integration

LECO ↔ G6 LIMS Bidirectional Instrument Integration

LECO's TruMac and TruSpec analyzers run the combustion-based proximate and ultimate analysis that determines coal quality — moisture, ash, volatile matter, and calorific value. Before this integration, results had to be manually keyed into G6 LIMS, a slow step and a common source of transcription error on high-volume test days.

Engineered a bidirectional data pathway between the analyzers and G6 LIMS using COM interface protocols: sample ID verification, real-time result import, and an automatic QC flag check that routes clean results straight to auto-import and only routes exceptions to an analyst — removing the manual re-entry step for the majority of samples.

03
Diagnostics · Support

Cross-Site Instrument Defect Resolution

Owned instrument integration troubleshooting across VBScript-based interfaces spanning three sites — the ManTech CSV-to-XML pipeline, the Shimadzu TOC-L CPH integration, and barcode-driven sample ID parsing — resolving data mapping and analyte override defects at Perth and Newburn.

Two defects stood out. First, a calculation error in surrogate recovery reporting traced back to VBScript's banker's-rounding behavior (rounding to the nearest even digit instead of standard arithmetic rounding), which was quietly skewing environmental compliance results. Second, a recurring Crystal Reports "Error 91" in the Portsmith Analytical Report, root-caused to dynamic analyte column counts exceeding the page's runtime boundary once a job had enough analytes.

Industry context: the Shimadzu TOC-L CPH measures organic carbon down to 4 µg/L using 680°C combustion catalytic oxidation — precise enough that a silent rounding bug can move a result across a regulatory compliance threshold without anyone noticing until an audit.

Custom EQuIS EDD Export Specification — Kleinfelder

EQuIS is the environmental and geotechnical data platform used by most US and Australian regulators and consultancies — over 400 analytical labs submit results into it via a standardised Electronic Data Deliverable (EDD) format so agencies can validate and load data automatically instead of re-keying it.

Built and iteratively refined a custom EQuIS EDD export specification for client Kleinfelder, mapping internal LIMS fields (sample ID, location, analyte, result, unit, matrix) to their EDD column equivalents, correcting SQL export logic, column mapping, and depth/matrix validation across more than 20 revisions as the client's data-delivery requirements evolved — landing on a repeatable, client-approved export now used as the account standard.
